Questions

Everything you wanted to know.

How is this different from Hers or any drugstore Women's Minoxidil?
Our protocols combine 3–4 actives in one routine, compounded to your trigger (postpartum, perimenopause, PCOS, family pattern). Your clinician actually adjusts the protocol at 90 days based on your progress photos — most drugstore brands hand you the same Minoxidil bottle for two years and call it done.
Is this FDA-approved?
Every active inside (Minoxidil, Spironolactone, Dutasteride, Tretinoin, Fluocinolone, Biotin, Ketoconazole) is FDA-approved individually. Compounded finished products aren't separately FDA-approved — compounding is the legal pharmacy process that combines them into one personalized formulation under USP 795/797 standards.
Is Spironolactone safe for women?
Used off-label for female pattern thinning for 30+ years. Generally well-tolerated; your clinician monitors potassium and adjusts dose. Contraindicated if you're pregnant, breastfeeding, or trying to conceive — we screen for this before prescribing.
I'm postpartum — is it safe to start now?
Postpartum shedding usually peaks 3–6 months after delivery and resolves on its own. If it's heavy or hasn't recovered by month 9–12, treatment is reasonable. If you're still breastfeeding, your clinician keeps you on Biotin and Minoxidil-based options and defers Spironolactone or Dutasteride until you've finished.
Will Minoxidil make me grow facial hair?
Topical Minoxidil rarely causes facial hair growth — usually only via hand or pillowcase transfer. Low-dose oral Minoxidil carries a small peach-fuzz risk; your clinician can lower the dose or switch you back to topical.
Is my information private?
Yes. Fully HIPAA-compliant. Your intake, messages, and shipping info are encrypted and never shared with insurance, employers, or marketing partners. Card statements read as a neutral DirectCare AI charge.
